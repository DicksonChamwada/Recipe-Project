Chicken Biriyani preparation

Swahili biriyani definitely deserves a place in the culinary hall of fame. Here is the easy version

Ingredients

  • 1 whole Chicken (Cut into 8 pieces)
  • 5 whole tomatoes (chopped)
  • 6 onions Sliced (Deep fried until golden and crispy)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1–2 tbsp Cumin (ground)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cloves (ground)
  • 1 tsp cardamom (ground)
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp ginger (crushed)
  • 1 tsp garlic (crushed)
  • 1 1/2 cup water
  • 3 potatoes (peeled; halved & deep fried)
  • 3 tbsp Coriander (roughly chopped)
  • 1 chilli (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup Mala (or natural yoghurt)

Instructions

  1. In a thick bottomed pan, add 1 tbsp ghee followed by the tomato paste and fry it for 1 min on medium heat.
  2. Add 1 tbsp cumin, 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon, 1 tsp ground cardamom, 1/2 tsp black pepper and a few cloves (4-5).
  3. Cook the spices for 2 min then add the chopped tomatoes and cover the pan.
  4. After 13 min, add the chicken, garlic, ginger, fresh chilly and, salt.
  5. Cover and cook until chicken soften (about 15min)
  6. Add 1/2 cup of mala or natural yoghurt cover and reduce the heat.
  7. Deep fry your sliced onions if you haven’t and also fry the potatoes until a skin forms.
  8. Add the deep friend and onions and potatoes to the biriyani. Cook until the potatoes are soft.
  9. Serve with rice

Beef Stew preparation

This beef steak is a very easy recipe to make. A tender cut of beef is sliced thin, then marinated with lemon juice and soy sauce for at least an hour.

Ingredients

  • 4kgs Beef steak, sliced thin
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp cloves (ground)
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped

Instructions

  1. Place sliced beef in a large bowl. Whisk together lemon juice, soy sauce, sugar, salt, and pepper in a small bowl; pour over beef and toss to coat. Stir in cornstarch. Cover and refrigerate for 1 hour to overnight.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Remove beef slices from marinade, shaking to remove any excess liquid. Discard marinade.
  4. Working in batches, fry beef slices in hot oil until they start to firm and are reddish-pink and juicy in the center, 2 to 4 minutes per side. Transfer beef slices to a serving platter.
  5. Heat olive oil in a small skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir onion and garlic in hot oil until onion is golden brown, 5 to 7 minutes; spoon over beef slices.

Slime-Okra preparation

Here is a recipe for what i like to call, ‘toasted okra’. I love adding onions, peppers and a touch of lemon to lift the whole dish..

Ingredients

  • 25 okra
  • 1 onion; cut in rings
  • 1/2 capsicum; diced
  • 2 tsp vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Cut the okra into small bite sizes.
  2. Heat a thick bottomed pan until smoking hot and add the okra.
  3. Keep mixing the okra so they don’t burn. Think of this as toasting, you want to toast the okra. You can add 1 tsp vegetable oil if you like.
  4. Cook for 8 min then set the okra aside.
  5. In the same pan, add the onion rings and capsicum. Again, you can add 1 tsp vegetable oil if you like. Cook for 1 min.
  6. Add minced garlic followed by black pepper. Cook for 1 min then add the lemon juice and cook for a minute.
  7. Remove from the flame and mix the okra and the onions together.
  8. Add salt to taste

Crispy Fried Fish preparation

Here is a recipe for what i like to call, Crispy Fried Fish..

Ingredients

  • 2 small/medium fish
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 3 onions
  • 5 cloves garlic; crushed
  • 1/2 inch ginger; crushed
  • 1/2 cup Soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 lemon; juiced

Instructions

  1. Score both sides of the fish (this gives you extra crispy bits).
  2. Add cooking oil into a large pan (i use a non stick pan).
  3. Heat the oil and add your whole fish.
  4. Fry for 4 min then gently turn the fish over.
  5. Cook the other side for 4 min as well.
  6. If you have a metal rack, place the fish on it to drain off any excess oil.
  7. In a separate non stick pan, add the onions and sauté for 8 min on medium flame.
  8. Add the ginger and garlic and cook for a further 2 min – You can add fresh chopped chilly at this point too
  9. Next, add in the say sauce, tamarind paste and sugar. Cook for 5 min or until mixture starts to bubble away.
  10. Add the lemon juice and taste it.
